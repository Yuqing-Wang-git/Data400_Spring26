Correct Specification of the Model

The project will evaluate how song structures (e.g., intro length, time-to-hook) vary between different streaming eras. Additionally, I will develop a predictive regression model to determine which specific audio features most strongly correlate with “virality” on TikTok versus “long-term streaming” on Spotify or YouTube. This multi-variable approach allows me to test the hypothesis that payout rules fundamentally influence song composition.

**Implications for Stakeholders

For content creators and labels, this research identifies structural “blueprints” that increase the probability of a song exceeding the 30-second payout threshold. Platform developers can use these insights to adjust recommendation algorithms based on how structural changes affect user retention and skip rates. Industry analysts will gain a deeper understanding of how economic “pay-per-play” models are shifting the financial value of recorded music.

Ethical, Legal, Societal Implications

This study examines the ethical risk of “algorithmic homogenization,” where creators may feel forced to prioritize commercial “hooks” over artistic diversity to survive financially. It highlights legal and fairness debates regarding current royalty models that may inadvertently disadvantage genres with traditionally longer compositions, such as classical or jazz. Ultimately, the project addresses the societal impact of the “attention economy,” investigating whether platform rules are permanently altering the way culture is produced and consumed.
